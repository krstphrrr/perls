Welcome to the Pole-to-Pole Ecological Research Lattice of Sites (P2ERLS) homepage! 

NOTE: This site is under construction

P2ERLS (pronounced pearls) is a portal that allows easy access to information about ecological research sites and links to their websites. Information can be found at four spatial scales: individual sites, logical gradients of sites within a region, and networks of sites located both within and across continents and the globe.

 
 
Click on the United States to see an example of future functionality. In the future, click on the map to dynamically find a site or ecological gradient by name, network, or  geographic region.

This “network of networks” is designed to: facilitate new research opportunities by assisting in the identification of individual sites and gradients of sites, and promote synthesis via increased interactions among researchers working at different sites. We will be adding sites both within North America and on other continents through time. If you would like to add a site, please contact us (see below).

P2ERLS is a collaboration among the Jornada Basin Long Term Ecological Research site (LTER) funded by the National Science Foundation, the USDA Agricultural Research Service (ARS), Jornada Experimental Range, and New Mexico State University; all located in Las Cruces, New Mexico, USA.
